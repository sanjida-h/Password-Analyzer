PASSWORD ANALYZER

Why 12+? Modern hardware can crack short passwords in seconds. Longer passwords are far harder to break.
The "Pwned" Check: Your password is checked against billions of leaked credentials to see if it has already been exposed.
Passphrases: Simple phrases like Robot Apple Sky are easier to remember and much harder to crack.
Length Matters: A longer password like coffeeTableWindow is stronger than a short complex one.
Password Reuse: Using the same password on multiple sites means one breach can unlock everything.
Brute Force: Attackers use powerful systems that can try billions of guesses every second.
Common Patterns: Passwords like Password123 or Welcome1 are cracked almost instantly.
Personal Info: Names, birthdays, or things like John2005 are easy for attackers to guess.
Keyboard Patterns: Sequences like qwerty or 123456 are among the first guesses.
Symbols Help: Adding symbols like ! or @ increases the number of possible combinations.
Password Managers: Tools can generate and store strong passwords so you do not have to remember them.
Dictionary Attacks: Attackers test huge lists of real words, not just random guesses.
Time to Crack: Adding just a few extra characters can turn seconds into years.
Case Sensitivity: Mixing uppercase and lowercase letters makes passwords harder to predict.
Data Breaches: Billions of real passwords are already exposed and reused in attacks.
Automation: Attacks run automatically and can test thousands of passwords in moments.
Unpredictability: The less your password looks like a real word, the safer it is.
Unique Passwords: Every account should have its own password to stay secure.
Real Risk: Most hacked accounts come from weak or reused passwords.
Try It Yourself: Enter common leaked passwords like 123456, password, or qwerty and watch how fast they are cracked.
